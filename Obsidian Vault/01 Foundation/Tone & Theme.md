# Tone & Theme

> **Purpose:** Establishes the political/social tone, the setting, and the stance the game takes. Critical for a politics-themed game to avoid being accidentally offensive or incoherent.
>
> **Depends on:** [[Vision Statement]], [[Player Fantasy]]
> **Feeds into:** [[Narrative & Flavor]], [[Visual & Audio Style]], [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]

## Tone (the register)

**Warm satire / gentle comedy.** Affectionate and knowing rather than biting. The game lovingly ribs the *apparatus* of campaigning — yard signs, robocalls, donor dinners, debate theater, consultants, polling — and ribs *every ideological archetype with equal affection*. It is funny *with* politics, not cruel *about* it.

- The eventual **galactic-republic reveal** (post-MVP) lands as a delightful absurd punchline, escalating the comedy, not turning dark.
- Emotional throughline still honors the [[Player Fantasy]]: the grassroots underdog is played sincerely even while the surrounding world is gently absurd.

## Setting

A **real-feeling but unnamed United States.** Recognizable U.S. civic structure and the explicit office ladder (city council → … → President), real-world interest-group categories, and real policy debates — but **no named real-world geography required**. The galactic republic (post-MVP) sits on top of this, revealed only at the ceiling.

## Political Stance — EVEN-HANDED SANDBOX *(the governing rule)*

**The game never editorializes about which politics are correct.** This is the single most important content rule, and every writer/designer/implementer must obey it:

- **The player freely chooses their ideology, per playthrough.** Ideology is a *costume the player picks*, not a fixed identity and not the designer's voice.
- **Every ideological path is equally winnable.** No position is mechanically "right" or "punished for being wrong." Balance must hold across the left-center-right spectrum.
- **Real ideologies and real interest groups are engaged directly** — labor unions, small business, farmers, etc. — and real policy debates appear. But they are presented as the player's *menu of choices*, never as the game's endorsements.
- **The satire targets the machinery and the archetypes, evenly.** If one side is teased, all sides are teased the same amount.

> The design goal: let players **live out their personal ideological dreams** in a sandbox that respects them, while the game stays neutral and funny.

## Core Mechanic This Establishes: Policy Promises & Interest Groups

Tone choice C surfaced a **core strategic system** (to be fully specified in section 04, likely its own note alongside [[Campaigns]]):

- **Policy promises are a trade-off engine.** Each promise courts some interest groups (gaining their voters/support) while alienating others.
- You **cannot please everyone** — building a winning coalition is the strategic heart of each election.
- Ideology is expressed *through* which promises you make and which groups you court.

## Sensitivity Boundaries

**Governing rule:** even-handedness above resolves most risk. **All content boundaries are now decided:**

- ✅ Real interest-group *categories* (unions, small business, farmers, etc.) — **in.**
- ✅ Real policy *issues/positions* across left-center-right — **in**, as player choices, never judged.
- ✅ **Parties: fictional but recognizable.** Invent parties with recognizable vibes and fictional names — **never** the real Democrat/Republican labels.
- ✅ **Real people: out.** No real politicians or public figures appear — not as characters, not even named in flavor/news text. All characters are fictional.
- ✅ **Identity hot-buttons (religion, race, abortion, guns, immigration): a few may appear as issues, handled even-handedly.** Most issues stay on economics/governance/process; a small number of recognizable hot-button issues may appear, written neutrally — no answer is "the correct politics." The satire still aims at the *machinery*, not at identities. → [[Narrative & Flavor]]

## Resolved Decisions & Tuning Targets

- ✅ **Governing/society layer cut from MVP** — the game is about *winning* offices; ideology lives in the campaign-time positioning trade-off. → [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]].
- ✅ **Content boundaries** (parties, real people, issue scope) — decided above.
- 🎯 **Balance target — "every ideology equally winnable":** an ongoing tuning constraint, enforced via symmetric bonus/malus packages and capped multipliers, validated in playtest. → [[Economy Model]], [[Number Scaling & Curves]], [[Pacing & Difficulty]].
- 🎯 **Warm-not-sharp editorial guardrail:** every line reviewed against the even-handed test in [[Narrative & Flavor]].
