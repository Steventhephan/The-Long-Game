# Progression Arc

> **Purpose:** The macro journey from the first tap to the endgame (President, for MVP) — the overall shape of growth and "becoming." Ties mechanical progression to the [[Player Fantasy]].
>
> **Depends on:** [[Player Fantasy]], [[Core Loop]], [[Generators]]
> **Feeds into:** [[Eras & Phases]], [[Prestige & Reset System]], [[Pacing & Difficulty]]

## The Spine

**Linear, 8 offices, two elections each — a primary then a general = 16 elections to the Presidency (MVP).**

> City Council → Mayor → County Council → County Executive → State Legislature → Governor → Senate → President
> *(each rung = Primary, then General)*

No branching: always the same sequence. (Branching is a possible post-MVP extension, enabled by the data-driven office design from [[Vision Statement]].)

## The Recurring Beat — Primary vs. General

Every rung is a two-beat squeeze on the player's **ideological position** (a measured radical↔moderate axis):

- **PRIMARY — lean to the base.** A narrower, more *radical* electorate; interest groups push the player toward their side's extreme. You win by leaning in — *but overcommitting sets a trap for the general.*
- **GENERAL — pivot to the center.** A broader, *moderate* electorate. You **can reposition toward the middle, but flip-flopping costs you** (lost interest-group support / credibility / cash, scaled to how far you swing — confirmed model C).

This "tack to the base, then pivot to the center" rhythm repeats at all 8 offices and **intensifies** as stakes rise (more interest groups, a wider primary/general gap). It is symmetric across the spectrum, so it stays even-handed (Pillar 5b). The flip-flop *cost* — never a wall — keeps the general winnable through effort (Pillar 5a).

## The Arc, in three movements

- **Beginning (City Council, Mayor).** Tiny pools, 1 rival, short timers. Tap + first Field/Finance generators. The intro underdog; systems are minimal so the player learns the loop.
- **Midgame (County, State Legislature).** Complexity layers in — interest groups, minigames, abilities, ideological forks, cross-track synergies, more rivals. Pools and timers grow. (Exact switch-on cadence → [[Eras & Phases]], [[Milestones & Unlocks]].)
- **Endgame / MVP climax (Governor → Senate → President).** National scale: capped-max rivals, longest timers, grand generators (National Media Team, Super PAC), deepest ideological positioning. The Presidential general is the climactic test and the v1 victory.

## What Changes Qualitatively (not just bigger numbers)

1. **New generators** unlock per office ([[Generators]]).
2. **New systems switch on** (interest groups → minigames → abilities → ideological forks).
3. **Rival count rises** (1 → capped maximum) — [[Opposition & Conflict]].
4. **The positional squeeze sharpens** — primaries get more radical, generals more demanding.
5. **Persistence vs. renewal:** the operation (generators, universal upgrades, Charisma, Volunteers) carries forward and compounds; **voters reset every election**, renewing the underdog feeling (Pillar 3).

## Resolved Decisions

- ✅ **Positional system specified** in [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]: **single left-right scalar**; **emergent party** (no up-front pick — your platform's lean sorts you); **flip-flop cost = escalating cash + eroding trust** (no new resource).
- ✅ **Lose-condition interaction (resolved in [[Prestige & Reset System]]):** losing *any* election (primary or general) ends the run → restart at City Council with banked Prestige. The sudden-death runoff ([[Core Loop]]) prevents leading-but-sub-50% losses.
- ✅ **Scope phased** in [[Implementation Roadmap]] — vertical slice = one office (primary + general), then widen.
