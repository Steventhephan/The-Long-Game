# Events & Crises

> **Purpose:** Dynamic interruptions that add variance and agency to the steady-state race — scandals, opportunities, dilemmas. The State-era hook; intensifies through Federal.
>
> **Depends on:** [[Core Loop]], [[Primary Resources]], [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]
> **Feeds into:** [[Opposition & Conflict]], [[Game Feel & Juice]], [[Pacing & Difficulty]]

## Two Forms (C — both)
- **Choice dilemmas** — the event pops, the **race pauses**, and the player picks a response with **bloc trade-offs** (e.g., a scandal breaks → *Deny / Apologize / Spin*, each hitting different blocs). Delivered like a [[Minigames|minigame]].
- **Struck modifiers** — the event simply *hits*: a temporary buff/debuff or a bloc swing, **no menu**, which the player must **out-play** with the loop and [[Campaigns & Abilities|abilities]].

## Valence (mix)
- **Negative** — scandals, gaffes, crises, a bloc revolt.
- **Neutral** — situational dilemmas with no clean "good" option; pick the least-bad.
- **Positive** — a viral moment, an endorsement, a fundraising windfall.

## Triggers (combination)
- **Random** — an ambient chance during elections.
- **State-triggered** — the player's situation shifts odds: heavy **flip-flopping** or **extreme radicalism** raises scandal chance; a **neglected bloc** can revolt; high **Charisma** can seed positive events.
- **Scheduled** — fixed beats, e.g., an **"October surprise"** near election day.

## Event Roster (starter set)
| Event | Form | Valence | Trigger | Effect / Choices |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Scandal Breaks** | dilemma | neg | state (heavy flip-flopping) | *Deny* (−Moderates) / *Apologize* (−base, +trust) / *Spin* (cash cost) |
| **Viral Moment** | modifier | pos | random | temporary conversion boost across blocs |
| **Endorsement Offer** | dilemma | pos | random | *Accept* (+bloc support, commits a stance) / *Decline* (+Charisma) |
| **Gaffe** | modifier | neg | state (low Charisma) | brief conversion penalty |
| **Economic Report** | dilemma | neutral | scheduled | *Take credit* / *Stay cautious* / *Blame opponent* (bloc-dependent) |
| **Rival Scandal** | modifier | pos | random (targets a rival) | the rival's conversion drops |
| **October Surprise** | dilemma | neg | scheduled (late) | a late hit with mitigation choices |
| **Grassroots Wave** | modifier | pos | state (high Volunteers) | temporary Volunteer/tap surge |
| **Donor Backlash** | dilemma | neg | state (over-using Galas/flip-flops) | manage the fallout |

## Rivals & Events
Events can strike **rivals too** — a rival scandal is *your* opportunity. (How events target the player vs. a rival vs. all → open below.)

## Pillar 5a Guarantee
Because there are **no defensive abilities** ([[Campaigns & Abilities]]), events are weathered through **choices, offense, boosts, or simply out-converting** — **never an unblockable wall.** Even a bad outcome must stay **recoverable through effort**. Scheduled late-election events especially must avoid the "lose at the buzzer through no fault" feel-bad. → [[Pacing & Difficulty]]

## Resolved Decisions & Tuning Targets
- **Event roster/content** — many written events; choice dilemmas must stay **even-handed** (Pillar 5b — no answer is "the correct politics"). → [[Narrative & Flavor]]
- ✅ **Positive events can't be farmed** — they're cooldown/cap-gated. 🎯 Exact state-triggers & probabilities tuned in playtest. → [[Number Scaling & Curves]], [[Pacing & Difficulty]]
- ✅ **Event targeting** is defined per event — some hit the player, some a specific rival, some all candidates. → [[Opposition & Conflict]]
- **Telegraphing & stacking** of struck modifiers (how shown, can they pile up). → [[UX & UI Layout]], [[Game Feel & Juice]]
