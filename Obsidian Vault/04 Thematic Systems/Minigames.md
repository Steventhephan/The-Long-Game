# Minigames

> **Purpose:** The choice-based set-pieces (debates, town halls, galas…) that build **Charisma** and shift **interest-group support** — and, for some, commit the player's **platform stances**. Strategic punctuation between bursts of the tap-loop.
>
> **Depends on:** [[Primary Resources]], [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]
> **Feeds into:** [[Eras & Phases]], [[Economy Model]], [[Narrative & Flavor]]

## Integration — A Mix (C)
- **Mandatory, scheduled minigames** woven into the climb (e.g., a **debate** before/during an election) — semi-mandatory beats you must play.
- **Optional minigames** the player spins up to grind Charisma / court blocs when they choose.
- They debut in the **County era** ([[Eras & Phases]]).

## Format
- **Choice-based.** The player is presented a scenario and picks responses.
- **The real-time race PAUSES during a minigame** — no twitch pressure; choices are strategic, mobile-friendly, and can be made unhurried.
- **Short** — a handful of decisions per minigame, suited to lean-in sessions ([[Target Audience & Platform]]).

## What Choices Do
A choice can do any of:
- **Move interest-group support** (court/alienate blocs — feeds the coalition in [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]).
- **Build Charisma** (→ scales Volunteer gain — [[Primary Resources]]).
- **Commit/shift platform stances** (esp. debates = taking positions *on the record*), which interacts with your ideology designation and the **flip-flop cost**.

## Pillar 4 Resolution
Mandatory minigames are part of the climb and carry **real coalition trade-offs**, so they're core strategic content — not throwaway side content. Because they **pause** the tap-loop, they don't compete with it for attention; they're a *different beat*, not a replacement. The active loop remains the soul.

## Minigame Types & Sample Scenarios
| Minigame | Mandatory? | Gating | Leans toward | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Debate** | yes (per election) | — | partisan blocs | **commits a stance on the record**; sways base; +Charisma |
| **Town Hall** | optional | cooldown + cash | moderate/independent | sways the center; +Charisma |
| **Fundraising Gala** | optional | cooldown + cash | donor blocs | cash + donor-bloc support |

**Sample — Debate** · *"On taxes, will you raise, hold, or cut?"*
- *"Raise on the wealthy to fund programs."* → commits Taxes = **L**; +Labor/Teachers, −Business; +Charisma
- *"Hold the line and balance the budget."* → commits Taxes = **C**; +Moderates; small +Charisma
- *"Cut taxes to grow the economy."* → commits Taxes = **R**; +Business/Energy, −Labor; +Charisma

**Sample — Town Hall** · *"A worried parent asks about schools."*
- *"Fully fund public schools."* → +Teachers/Unions
- *"Improve schools within budget."* → +Moderates/Seniors
- *"Expand school choice."* → +Faith/Business, −Teachers

**Sample — Fundraising Gala** · *"A major donor offers a big check — with strings."*
- *"Accept gladly."* → large cash + that donor bloc; small −Civil-Liberties (optics)
- *"Accept, stay independent."* → moderate cash; neutral
- *"Politely decline."* → +Charisma (integrity); no cash

## Resolved Decisions & Tuning Targets
- ✅ **Free-Charisma-farm (resolved):** optional minigames are gated by a **cooldown + cash cost** (mandatory debates are free), and choices carry real bloc trade-offs — no cost-free grinding.
- ✅ **Debate answers formally commit platform stances** (so reversing them later triggers the flip-flop cost); other minigames (town halls, galas) only nudge support/Charisma. → [[Policy, Platform & Interest Groups]]
- **Roster & unlock cadence** — which types appear at which era.
- ✅ **Starter scenarios authored** (samples above, even-handed). 🎯 full scenario library is ongoing content (Phase 6). → [[Narrative & Flavor]]
- **Balance:** Charisma/support values per choice. → [[Number Scaling & Curves]]
